# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People living with neurological conditions, patients preparing for rehabilitation, family members, and caregivers comparing rehabilitation centers in Italy. Users may be stressed, time-constrained, unfamiliar with clinical terminology, or navigating the service on a phone while coordinating care.

## Product Purpose

Neurovia helps people discover and compare neurological rehabilitation centers using verified institutional information and transparent community feedback. The initial directory covers Italy, while its geography, taxonomy, and data model are designed to expand across Europe without changing the core experience.

Success means a user can quickly identify plausible centers, understand why each may fit their needs, compare relevant care dimensions, and contribute useful experience reports without mistaking community information for medical advice.

## Brand Personality

Serious, trustworthy, human, and exceptionally easy to navigate. The voice is calm and direct, explains clinical terms in plain language, and never sensationalizes health outcomes.

## Anti-references

Avoid sterile hospital administration software, commercial “top ten clinic” listicles, tourism-booking interfaces, generic SaaS dashboards, unexplained composite scores, fabricated testimonials, visual clutter, and claims that imply guaranteed medical outcomes.

## Design Principles

1. Make the next useful decision obvious, especially for stressed users.
2. Separate verified facts, community experience, and missing information visibly.
3. Explain every score and never rank a center without enough review evidence.
4. Use clinical precision without institutional jargon.
5. Design every taxonomy, filter, and record for expansion from Italy to Europe.

## Trust & Contribution Model

Anyone may browse. Email-verified accounts may submit reviews and propose new centers. Submissions require a treatment-relationship declaration, an approximate treatment period, acceptance of community rules, and moderation before publication. Centers may publish identified responses but cannot suppress critical reviews.

The community score is shown out of 10 and uses Bayesian weighting so small review counts do not dominate rankings. Category scores remain visible for medical team, neurological specialization, rehabilitation technology and robotics, nursing, facilities, communication, and discharge support. Data completeness is reported separately from care quality.

New center submissions require an official website and enter a moderation queue with duplicate detection.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Use semantic structure, full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, strong contrast, non-color-dependent status communication, 44px minimum touch targets, understandable form errors, reduced-motion support, browser zoom, and responsive layouts. Avoid cognitive overload and provide plain-language explanations for medical and scoring terms.
